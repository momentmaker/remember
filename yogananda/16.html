Don’t waste the perception of the God’s presence,
acquired in meditation,
by useless chatting.
Idle words are like bullets:
they riddle the milk pail of peace.
In devoting time unnecessarily to conversation and exuberant laughter,
you’ll find you have nothing left inside.
Fill the pail of your consciousness with the milk of meditative peace,
then keep it filled.
Joking is false happiness.
Too much laughter riddles the mind and lets the peace in the bucket flow out,
wasting it.
