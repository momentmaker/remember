It is unrealistic as well as eventually injurious to believe that other people “should” adopt and live by one’s own personal standards, morals, and code of conduct as well as interpretation of reality. Projected moralism is always expressed as “should” and often leads to resentment, hatred, grudges, or even retaliatory vengeance—and, of course, war (such as the naïve American view that all other nations “should” be democracies). One can, by choice, reject the temptation to habitual judgmentalism. The result is a great inner peace.
